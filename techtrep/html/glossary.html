Glossary of Terms

Announcements - Important communication from Tech Trep Academy about key events, program highlights, and policy updates. All announcements for the year are archived in InfoCenter under Announcements and may be reviewed at any time.

Bill.com - The service used by Tech Trep Academy to reimburse families AFTER they have filled out the reimbursement request and uploaded their receipts into InfoCenter. This is secure service allows Tech Trep Academy to reimburse families safely. Parents should NEVER send receipts to Bill.com. Reimbursements submitted through InfoCenter.

Canvas - A safe and secure online learning platform where students access Tech Trep Academy Direct Tech courses

Canvas-based Tech Class - Online Tech Trep Academy Tech and Entrepreneurship elective courses accessed by the student directly inside of Canvas

Concurrent Enrollment - When a student takes college AND High School classes.

Custom-built - Building a course in Schedule Builder around with student and parent selected materials. May include learning resources, private tutoring/lessons, and other educational supplies are purchased by parents.

Direct Purchase - Tech Trep Academy will purchase requested supplemental resources directly for a student through Amazon, Rainbow Resources, or other providers. Families sign up for zoom meetings with an order specialist.

Dual Enrollment - With approval, students can take on-site classes at their local district school. This greatly reduces the student’s reimbursement allowance since funding is shared between two schools. Each year long course at a local school will use at least $340 of a student’s supplemental learning fund. This $340 is for the full year ($170/semester), and is automatically withdrawn at the beginning of the year. Contact Amber Cornwell if you are looking into this option.

Enrollment Packet - Necessary documentation to enroll your student in this program. Uploaded into Power School website. Includes: Proof of Residency, Birth Cert, Vaccination/Opt-out Documents

Field Trips - Tech Trep Academy organizes fun and educational field trips for our students. FREE for students and one parent. Other guests receive a discount. Families may attend field trips throughout the state. Information is found in InfoCenter under Calendar & Notifications.

Homeroom Resources - Unlimited FREE access to core curriculum which maps to Idaho Core Standards (i.e. Khan Academy, BrainPop/BP Jr., Generation Genius, etc.) Discounted costs for many other resources (Reading Eggs, ALEKS, Accelerated Reader, IXL, etc.)

Homeroom Teacher - An Idaho-certified teacher who reviews each student’s Bi-weekly Learning Plans and Assessments and provides personalized feedback and support to the family. One teacher is assigned to each family with K-8 students and this person should be the first and main point of contact for the family.

Idaho Digital Learning Alliance (IDLA) - Not to be confused with IHLA! Provides virtual, dual-credit and advanced placement courses for middle school and High School students.

Idaho Home Learning Academy (IHLA) - The Idaho school that partners with Tech Trep Academy to provide funds, teachers, special education services to home school families across Idaho. Tech Trep Academy is a program offered through IHLA.

Idaho Reading Assessment (IRI) - An early reading screener and diagnostic assessment required for all K-3 grade students in Idaho each Fall and Spring. Tech Trep Teachers proctor this assessment virtually.

InfoCenter - The main hub of our online program, a place for parents to get information, submit Learning plans and assessments as well as reimbursement receipts.

InfoCenter Parent Calendar - A singular location in InfoCenter where you can view all program-related deadlines, holidays, and events

Idaho Standards Achievement Test (ISAT) - All students in grades 3-10 are required to take the ISAT test each year. The test is used in the assessment of a school's effectiveness.

Learning Assessment - One formative, summative or authentic assessment is submitted for each of the 4 core subject areas every two weeks. These “assessments” are learning samples which can be in the form of a screenshot of a progress report or quiz from an online program, a report, a picture of a worksheet, a video of a student describing the LG, etc. There are many forms these “assessments” can take. Submitted through Infocenter

Learning Plan - An outline of the family learning plan submitted by the parent at the beginning of each semester. Every two weeks the parent will indicate that the plan is still accurate or that it is being revised. Parents can request additional resources or support also. Submitted through Infocenter

Need Help Form - A form (also accessible through InfoCenter) to request help from a teacher or other Tech Trep Academy personnel.

Oneida School District #351 - Home of IHLA located in Malad Idaho. Tech Trep Academy is a program offered through IHLA.

On-Site Programs - Programs that partner or are sponsored by TTA that provide part-time, on-site classes for students enrolled in Tech Trep Academy. These include: TTA Bridge (Nampa), Vineyard, and Garnet Prep.

Optional Homeroom Resources - These programs are available for request in Homeroom. Each program has a fee that will be deducted directly from the student’s supplemental learning resources. A list of available programs is in InfoCenter under the HOMEROOM tab.

Learning Goals - Otherwise known as LG’s, these learning goals are aligned with the Idaho state standards in math, language arts, science, and social studies. Each semester, a student will upload 8 “assessments” to show “mastery” of the LG’s.

Request for Reimbursement - Submit for reimbursement through InfoCenter for approved Supplemental Learning resources. Required items: Form and Receipts. Last day to submit receipts is April 30th Submitted through Infocenter

Schedule Builder - A tool inside InfoCenter that allows you to indicate what programs you are choosing to participate in (by subject and by curriculum provider). Submitted through Infocenter

School Links - The platform used by the High School students to build their 4 year plans.

School of Enrollment - IHLA is the school of enrollment for Tech Trep Academy Students.

Schoology - The Learning management system used by the TTA Jr. High Virtual & all High School teachers to deliver and manage their classes.

Supplemental Learning Resources - Each student in grades 1–11 has access to $1700 ($850 for kindergarteners) that can be used for the supplemental learning resources.

SST/SPED - Student Success Team (TTA)/Special Education Department (IHLA)

Tech Trep Academy Direct - A course that is paid for and provided directly to the student by Tech Trep Academy (vs. paid by a parent and reimbursed)

Truancy Letter - A formal letter sent to the parents from the Principal of IHLA due to inactivity in submitting Learning plans and Assessment. Truancy letters may result in removal from the program, restriction of funds and/or inability to re-enroll in the program for the next school year.

Semester Elective - Each student in grades K-8 will report once a semester about any elective learning they have completed. This is a very broad elective which can include subjects such as Home Ec, Health, dance, marshal arts, engineering, LEGO robotics, sewing, photography, typing, music, PE, foreign languages, etc.

Withdrawals - Removal from the program, either voluntarily or when it appears that it’s not a good fit for your student; review Withdrawal Policy details in the IHLA Handbook. Submitted through Infocenter
